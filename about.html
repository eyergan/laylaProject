Hello, my name is Layla Zaki Lakos! I grew up in Dubai, UAE in an American-Egyptian-Hungarian family. I now live halfway across the world with my husband in Seattle. Sadly, I don't have any pets but I do feed the crows in my alleyway.

I am currently working as a metal fabricator and a video editor. It's a surprise to me too... my previous work experience was in farming, mediation, and advocacy.

I love to draw comics (because I like pictures and words). I find a lot of inspiration in my Islamic upbringing and the practice of calligraphy (making words beautiful). Right now, I am working on a couple of projects that have to do with my Arab American identity. I spent a long time thinking that I'd better not share my thoughts because I didn't feel like a good representation of what a Muslim or Arab woman looked or acted like.

I've been lucky to meet many other multicultural people and have been sharing more. I am not trying to represent anything other than my own experience, which takes the pressure off of 'getting it right.'

I fell in love with welding in college, where I became good friends with the Safety Director of the Sculpture Department. I learned how to weld. My first project was a slammable, lockable, functional door, that I later placed in the woods with the help of my friends. I have always oriented myself towards projects that serve some kind of function (although that is up to interpretation). My skills include: soldering / finishing / chainmaille sand casting / tig welding mig welding / general fabrication

I would love to have more artistic collaboration in my life! It's the best way for me to learn, and refocuses art on shared experiences. That could mean creating a project, swapping art, skill sharing, or crafting something to support an organization.
